Dosa

Description

A dosa is a thin pancake or crepe originating from South India, made from a fermented batter predominantly consisting of lentils and rice. It is somewhat similar to a crepe in appearance, although savoury flavours are generally emphasized (sweet variants also exist). Its main ingredients are rice and black gram, ground together in a fine, smooth batter with a dash of salt, then fermented. Dosas are a common dish in South Indian cuisine, but now have become popular all over the Indian subcontinent. Dosas are served hot along with chutney by tradition and sambar in recent times. Other accompaniments include chutney powder (a fine groundnut and lentil powder).

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Urad Dal
  • Chana Dal
  • Water
  • methi seeds

Steps

  1. Add half a cup urad dal, 2 tablespoon chana dal and half a teaspoon of methi seeds
  2. Add one and a half cup rice to another pot
  3. Rinse dals thoroughly a few times and soak in lots of water for 4 hours. During cold weather they can be soaked for 6 hours or overnight.
  4. Rinse rice too a few times until the water runs clear. Soak in enough water as mentioned above
  5. 30 mins before blending the batter, rinse and soak 2 tablespoons poha with quarter cup water for 30 mins.
  6. Add soaked poha to the grinder first. Drain the water completely and add half a teaspoon of salt and 3/4th cup of water.
  7. Blend until smooth forthy and bubbly.
  8. Add the rice batter to the urad dal batter and mix well.
  9. Ferment the batter untill it rises and becomes bubbly.
  10. Refrigerate once fermented.
  11. Add a few drops of oil on the pan and stir the batter on the pan and toast.
  12. Your Dosa is ready to serve.
